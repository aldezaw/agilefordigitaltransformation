Problem Statement

Nova's employees know AI is coming — but they aren't moving. They're waiting for the perfect plan, the guaranteed outcome, and the right moment. The real blocker isn't the technology. It's a mindset that treats uncertainty as a reason to pause, instead of a signal to run a small experiment.

How It Works

One forum. Four card types. A new habit.

Every week, each employee posts 20 cards across four colours — 5 of each type — in their department's forum thread. They end the week with a short reflection post. That's it. That's the whole product.

01
Join your track
Pick your function — Claims, HR, Finance, or CX. Your forum thread is already there with a new topic each week.
02
Post 20 cards a week
5 Fear Cards, 5 Action Cards, 5 Experiment Cards, 5 Learning Cards. Each one is a short post in the forum thread.
03
Earn points
Points vary by card type and format — text, tutorial, or video clip. The more you contribute and the richer the format, the more you earn.
04
Weekly reflection
Week closes with one reflection post. What worked, what didn't, what you're trying next. This unlocks the completion badge.
05
Build the habit
After 10 completed weeks, the habit is formed. Spark steps back. The tools — and the mindset — stay.
The Card System

Four colours. One complete learning cycle.

Each colour represents a step in the fear-to-action loop. Every week, employees must complete all four — no skipping straight to experiments without naming the fear first.

Fear Card — Red
Name the blocker
What's stopping you from trying AI this week? Name it honestly. Fear cards are never judged — they're the starting point.
  • "I'm afraid AI will make an error in a claim and I'll get blamed."
  • "I don't trust the output for anything client-facing."
  • "I don't have time to figure out a new tool right now."
Action Card — Blue
Respond to the fear
What's one thing you or your manager can do to unblock that fear? Action cards are the bridge from stuck to moving.
  • "Let's agree AI drafts only — human reviews before any claim is filed."
  • "Let's run a 10-minute test together, not alone."
  • "I'll do one small task today, not a full workflow change."
Experiment Card — Green
Document what you tried
What specific AI task did you actually run this week? Be concrete — tool used, what you asked it to do, how long it took.
  • "Used the claims summariser on 3 real notes. Took 4 minutes."
  • "Asked AI to draft a reply to a complaint. Edited before sending."
  • "Generated a weekly report outline. Saved about 20 minutes."
Learning Card — Yellow
Capture what you learned
What worked, what didn't, and what you'd do differently. Learning cards are how the whole team gets smarter together.
  • "The summary was accurate but missed tone — I'll add tone instructions next time."
  • "Faster than expected. Will use every Monday going forward."
  • "It struggled with our internal jargon. Need to pre-define terms."

Weekly Challenge Tracks

Role-specific. Concrete. Time-boxed.

Each function has its own 10-week challenge track. Topics escalate in confidence level — starting with low-stakes solo experiments and building toward team-level workflow changes.

Management View

Visibility without surveillance

Three dashboard levels — team, department, executive. All show group-level momentum. Individual compliance tracking is deliberately excluded. Managers see who needs a nudge, not a report card.

Implementation Timeline

18-month rollout. Three phases.

Phase 1 proves the concept with willing adopters before pushing company-wide. Each phase builds on evidence, not faith.

1
Seed
Months 1–3 · ~200 early adopters
  • Launch to AI Champions + volunteer teams in Bangkok and KL
  • Validate card tracks for Claims, CX, and Finance
  • Test manager dashboard with 5 team leads
  • Collect qualitative data on what's working
  • Identify 10 first-win stories for Phase 2 comms
2
Scale
Months 4–9 · all 5 offices
  • Full rollout across Bangkok, KL, Jakarta, Phnom Penh, Shanghai
  • Expand tracks to HR and Underwriting
  • Launch cross-office leaderboard and community feed
  • Integrate forum login with Nova SSO
  • First monthly management report to department heads
3
Embed & Retire
Months 10–18 · retire the scaffold
  • Spark Champions mentor new joiners directly — no more guided tracks
  • Forum evolves into peer-led AI community, not structured challenge
  • Management dashboards handed to HR as ongoing metric
  • Spark's structured card system formally decommissioned
  • The habit remains. The scaffold leaves.
The Principle That Matters

Most tools are designed to maximise engagement.
Spark is designed to make itself unnecessary.

Every other tool in Nova's transformation stack exists permanently. Spark's job is to get those tools used — then step back.

It's an on-ramp, not a destination. Once the habit of running weekly AI experiments is formed — roughly 10 completed weeks — the structured challenge system retires. The forum stays as a peer community. The cards go away.
The measure of success is when people stop needing Spark. If employees are still posting cards in year two because Spark is the only place AI happens at Nova, it failed. Success looks like AI showing up in Monday stand-ups, not forum threads.
This is what separates it from every other tool pitch. Nobody in a procurement meeting says "and here's how our product exits gracefully when it's no longer needed." Spark does. That's the design philosophy, and it's the one thing worth protecting in any presentation of this work.
