Problem statement

Nova's employees know AI is coming but aren't moving — because they're waiting for the perfect plan, the guaranteed outcome, and the right moment. The real blocker isn't the technology. It's a mindset that treats uncertainty as a reason to pause, instead of a signal to run a small experiment.

The solution

Nova Spark — your weekly AI experiment companion

A lightweight, habit-building tool deployed across all five Nova offices. Employees pick one role-specific AI challenge per week, try it, and share what happened. The tool teaches agile thinking by making you live it — not by explaining it.

Why this isn't just another tool on the portal

The training portal failed because it gave people knowledge without action. The AI tools failed because nobody connected them to a specific task on a specific Tuesday. Spark's job is to be the bridge — not a destination in itself, but the on-ramp to everything Nova has already built.

Every session ends with something the employee actually did — not watched or read
Challenges are role-specific and time-boxed — no abstract "explore AI" prompts
Failure is designed in — the "that didn't work" path is as clear as the success path
Spark retires once the habit is formed — it's not a permanent product, it's a scaffold
It links out to Nova's existing tools — it never duplicates them
Management visibility is built in — progress visible without surveillance

Challenges & habit formation

How many challenges to build a habit?

Based on habit formation research (66 days average), Spark targets 10 challenges over 12 weeks per employee — roughly one per week with buffer. The path is personalised by job function. Select a function to see the challenge track.

Implementation timeline

18-month rollout across Nova

Three phases. Each builds on the last. Phase 1 proves the concept with willing adopters before pushing company-wide.

1
Seed
Months 1–3 · ~200 early adopters
  • Launch Spark to AI Champions + volunteer teams in Bangkok and KL
  • Build and validate challenge library for Claims, CX, and Finance functions
  • Test manager dashboard with 5 team leads
  • Collect qualitative data: what's working, what's not
  • Identify 10 "first win" stories to socialise in Phase 2
2
Scale
Months 4–9 · all 5 offices
  • Full rollout across Bangkok, KL, Jakarta, Phnom Penh, Shanghai
  • Expand challenge library to HR, Underwriting, Technology functions
  • Launch team leaderboard and cross-office community feed
  • Integrate Spark login with Nova SSO (no new accounts)
  • First monthly management report issued to department heads
  • CEO town hall features 3 Spark stories from different countries
3
Embed
Months 10–18 · retire the scaffold
  • Spark habit data feeds into performance review conversations (optional)
  • Deep integration: Spark challenges linked directly to Nova tool portal
  • Agentic AI teams self-nominate based on Spark completion milestones
  • Spark challenge library maintained by AI Champions, not central IT
  • Evaluate sunset: if habit formed, Spark steps back — tools take over

Phase 1 · Months 1–3
Standalone Spark
Spark runs as a separate web app. Manual role setup. No SSO. Challenges are a curated static library. Manager dashboard is a simple weekly email report.
  • Web app (mobile-responsive)
  • 30 challenges across 3 functions
  • Weekly email digest to managers
Phase 2 · Months 4–9
Connected Spark
SSO integration removes friction. Challenges link directly to Nova's tool portal. Real-time dashboard replaces email. Cross-office feed goes live.
  • SSO login via Nova identity
  • 70+ challenges across 6 functions
  • Live manager + dept head dashboard
  • Direct links to AI tool portal
Phase 3 · Months 10–18
Embedded Spark
Spark habit data informs agentic AI team selection. Champions maintain the challenge library via API. Spark begins stepping back as tools take over.
  • Agentic team eligibility via milestones
  • Champion-editable challenge API
  • Sunset evaluation at month 18
